# üî∑ 1. Understanding Your Offer Letter  
Every offer has three critical components:

### **1. Funding Package**
Look for:
- guaranteed years of funding  
- stipend amount  
- TA/RA obligations  
- summer support (very important)  
- tuition + fee waivers  
- health insurance  

A strong offer includes:
- 5‚Äì6 years guaranteed funding (U.S.)  
- 3‚Äì4 years + dissertation-year options (UK/EU)  

If your offer does *not* mention summer funding: **assume it is NOT included.**

---

### **2. Advisor Assignment (U.S. vs UK/EU)**

#### **In the U.S.:**
- Advisor is often *not final* until after coursework.  
- The letter may list a ‚Äúprovisional advisor‚Äù ‚Äî this is normal.

#### **In the UK/EU:**
- Advisor *is* assigned at admission.  
- This decision determines 80% of your trajectory.  
- If concerns exist, raise them **before** accepting.

---

### **3. Program Expectations**
Look for:
- teaching load (TA hours per semester)  
- lab rotation requirements  
- milestone deadlines (qualifying exams, proposal, etc.)  
- publication expectations  

You should save the offer letter indefinitely ‚Äî it is a binding agreement.

# üî∑ 2. How to Compare Multiple Offers (Evidence-Based Method)
Use a 5-category scoring model:

### **1. Advisor Quality (40%)**
The single most predictive factor in PhD completion and success is your advisor.

Evaluate:
- mentorship style  
- publication track record  
- funding stability  
- graduation outcomes of previous students  
- alignment with your topic  

### **2. Research Environment (20%)**  
Consider:
- lab culture  
- department support  
- cohort strength  
- available datasets/resources  

### **3. Funding Stability (20%)**
Higher stipends do not always mean higher *quality of life*.  
Cost of living matters.

### **4. Program Prestige (10%)**  
Prestige matters **less** for PhDs than for MBAs ‚Äî but:
- placements  
- advisor reputation  
- field standing  
do influence academic careers.

### **5. Life Considerations (10%)**
Where you live for 5‚Äì7 years *matters*:
- family  
- visa conditions  
- mental health support  
- city affordability  

Make your decision based on **fit**, not ego.

# üî∑ 3. Should You Negotiate Your PhD Offer?
PhD offers are not like industry jobs.

### **You can ethically negotiate when:**
- you have multiple funded offers  
- you are choosing between comparable programs  
- another program offers a substantially higher stipend  
- you have childcare or relocation needs  

### **You should NOT negotiate:**
- if your offer is standard for that program  
- if the department explicitly forbids adjustments  
- by threatening or leveraging faculty inappropriately
