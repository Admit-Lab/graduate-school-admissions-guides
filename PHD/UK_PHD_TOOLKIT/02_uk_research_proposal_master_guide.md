# ğŸ”· 2. Supervisor Psychology: How They Read Your Proposal

UK supervisors read your proposal primarily through the lens of *risk*.

They ask:

- **Will this student be too dependent on me?**  
- **Is this methodology clear enough?**  
- **Is the scope too large or too vague?**  
- **Can this realistically be completed in 3 years?**  
- **Does this fit what I do? Can I supervise this without stretching myself?**  
- **Is this student ready, or will I spend the first year fixing their foundations?**  

Supervisors take on very few doctoral students â€” often only one new student every 1â€“2 years â€” so the bar is extremely high.

# ğŸ”· 3. The Three Questions Committees Use to Evaluate You

Every panel uses the same three-question heuristic.

### **1. Is the project WORTH doing?**  
(Original, meaningful, and fits contemporary scholarly debates.)

### **2. Is the project DOABLE in 3 years?**

If your proposal fails even one of these, you will be rejected.

Your abstract should summarize:

## **Section 3: Background & Context**

Explain:

- the field or domain  
- the problem space  
- why this topic matters now  
- how your project sits within existing academic debates  

You are not telling your personal story.  
You are showing your literacy in the field.

**Include:**  
- key authors  
- major theories  
- essential context  
- institutional, historical, or structural dynamics

## **Section 4: Research Problem + Rationale**

This is where you make your case.

You must answer:

**1. What is the problem?**  
**2. Why is this a problem?**  
**3. Why now?**  
**4. Why will your approach address it differently?**

Committees want clarity, not ambition.

Strong applicants remove unnecessary complexity.

---

## **Section 5: Research Questions + Objectives**

### **Research Questions:**  
Precise, feasible, answerable in 3 years.

### **Objectives:**  
The measurable steps you will take to answer each question.

**Example structure:**

**RQ1:** What is X?  
**Objective 1:** Develop a conceptual framework for understanding X.

**RQ2:** How does X influence Y?  
**Objective 2:** Conduct qualitative interviews to identify patterns.

**RQ3:** How can these findings inform Z?  
**Objective 3:** Build a model/testing mechanism.

**A strong mini-review will:**
